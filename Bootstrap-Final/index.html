About This Project

We started this project to take a look at Harrisonburg's historical houses, and how they've been preserved — or destroyed — over the years. Starting with JMU's campus, we take a look at how Rockingham County has developed since the late 1800s and the university's founding in 1908.

Explore the website to learn about some of Harrisonburg's most famous historical structures, including the recently demolished Haas House, the preserved Dallard Newman House and two locations currently at risk: the P. Bradley & Sons foundry and the Shoppes at Mauzy.
